Characters:
* Eleanor Vance, Senior Manager
* Mark Chen, Senior Data Analyst
Setting: A private conference room. Eleanor has a document on the table.
Duration: 15 minutes

(The conversation begins. Mark enters the room and sits down. Eleanor closes the door.)
Eleanor (2 minutes): "Hi Mark. Thank you for coming in. I know this isn't an easy conversation, but I want to be direct and transparent with you. As we’ve discussed in our last two check-ins, your performance has fallen short of expectations, specifically over the last six months. We’ve talked about the quality and timeliness of your reports and the impact it's having on the team's projects. We've also reviewed the key performance indicators for your role, which show a consistent dip.
After consulting with Human Resources and reviewing your performance data, we’ve made a decision. The purpose of this meeting is to formally present you with a Performance Improvement Plan, or PIP. I have a copy here for you, and one for us to review together."
(Eleanor slides a printed document across the table to Mark.)
Mark (4 minutes): "A PIP? I… I honestly didn't think it would come to this. I've been trying. The workload has been heavier, and I've had to manage multiple priorities for different teams, not just my own. I feel like I'm stretched thin. Is it really that bad?"
Eleanor: "I understand you feel that way, Mark, and I hear your concerns. We've taken your feedback into account. However, we're seeing tangible results that are impacting our ability to deliver. For example, the Q2 revenue analysis report was submitted three days late, which delayed the executive review meeting. Also, the data in your last two client-facing dashboards had minor but significant errors that required the junior team members to go back and fix them. These are not isolated incidents.
This isn't about blaming you; it's about addressing the core issues so you can get back on track. We've identified two key areas in this plan: data accuracy and project timeliness."
Mark: "But I've been with the company for seven years. I've always been a high performer. Why is this being treated as such a critical issue now?"
Eleanor: "Your past contributions are why we're so invested in helping you succeed, Mark. This decision wasn't made lightly. The company values your institutional knowledge and experience. However, the expectations for your role have evolved. We need to see a consistent level of performance that aligns with the senior analyst position. The current performance is not sustainable, and it's putting a strain on the team."
Eleanor (5 minutes): "This plan is a formal roadmap to help you meet those expectations. Let's look at the document together."
(Eleanor points to the sections on the PIP document.)
Eleanor: "The plan has two key objectives.
1. Data Accuracy: For the next 30 days, all your data analysis must pass a peer review with a zero-error rate. We will assign a peer to review your work before you submit it.
2. Project Timeliness: You will deliver your weekly reports and assigned dashboards by their specified deadlines. We will implement a new project management system to help you track your progress, and I will check in with you every three days to discuss your workload and any roadblocks you're facing.
This plan will be in effect for 60 days, beginning today. We will meet weekly to review your progress. My goal is for you to be successful, and I want you to use me as a resource. Human Resources is also a resource for you if you need to discuss this privately or confidentially."
Mark: "And what happens if I don't meet these goals?"
Eleanor (3 minutes): "That's a very important question. As the plan states, failure to meet the objectives may result in disciplinary action, up to and including the termination of your employment."
(A moment of silence as the weight of the statement settles in.)
Eleanor: "This is a serious matter, Mark, but it's also a clear path forward. We've provided the goals, the support, and a specific timeline. I'm here to support you through this process, but the commitment to improve must come from you. Do you understand the terms of the plan?"
Mark: "Yes, I understand."
Eleanor (1 minute): "Do you have any questions about the plan or the next steps?"
Mark: "No... not right now."
Eleanor: "Alright. We'll sign this document to acknowledge that we've had this conversation and that you understand the plan. Let's schedule our first check-in for Wednesday morning. We’ll meet here at 9 a.m. to review your first task. I’m confident we can work together to turn this around. I’ll send a calendar invite shortly. Thank you, Mark."
